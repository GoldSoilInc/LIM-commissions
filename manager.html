How Pay Is Calculated
Team pay + manager pay, defined

1 · Reward Bands

Every pre-qualified lead pays out a dollar reward. The size of that reward is set by the phone sourcer’s ratio for the period:

Ratio = that person’s campaign members ÷ their pre-qualified leads (as phone sourcer)

A lower ratio (fewer members needed per pre-qualified lead) earns a higher reward:

Ratio Total / lead Text share Phone share
Below 600$4.00$1.00$3.00
600 – 700$3.00$1.00$2.00
700 – 800$1.50$0.50$1.00
Above 800$0.00——

A ratio above 800 forfeits everything for that person — including any text credits they would have earned on other people’s leads. The forfeited money is not shifted to anyone else.

A lead counts as “pre-qualified” when its Date Pre-Qualified cell (column L) holds a real date — regardless of Lead Status (a nurture lead can still be pre-qualified). A blank or a placeholder dash (“-”) does not count.

2 · Pre-Qualify $

Pre-Qualified Lead — a lead sent to the Comps team by the LAMs for analysis.

For each pre-qualified lead, the reward is split between the two people who worked it:

  • The Text sourcer earns the Text share.
  • The Phone sourcer earns the Phone share.

Both shares are read from the phone sourcer’s band — the caller’s ratio sets the price of the lead. If the same person both texted and called a lead, they earn the full band amount. If the phone sourcer’s ratio is above 800, the whole lead pays $0 and the texter’s credit is forfeited too.

A person’s Pre-Qualify $ = the sum of all their text shares + phone shares across the period.

Reading the lead breakdown:

  • 🏆 Trophy — a trophy beside a lead’s name marks it pre-qualified. A lead with no trophy is a still-nurturing warm transfer and earns $0.
  • CALL (call credit) — you were the phone sourcer on a pre-qualified lead that someone else texted; you earn the phone share of that lead’s band.
  • TEXT CREDIT (text credit) — you were the text sourcer on a pre-qualified lead that someone else called; you earn the text share.
  • A pre-qualified lead you both texted and called shows with no label and earns the full band amount.

3 · Targets

Targets are pre-qualified-lead goals, scaled to the number of campaign members (more members → more pre-qualified leads expected). Both are rounded down:

  • Top Target = members ÷ 600
  • Optimal = members ÷ 700

Hitting Top Target means a strong conversion — few members needed per pre-qualified lead, which is also the band that pays the most per lead.

4 · Weekly Bonus Target

Each week the team has a pre-qualified goal that unlocks the weekly bonus:

Weekly Bonus Target = the team’s members that week ÷ 700 (rounded down) — the Optimal goal.

If the team’s weekly pre-qualified count reaches Top Target (members ÷ 600) instead, the bonus rate goes up (see section 6).

5 · How to Qualify for the Weekly Bonus

Three conditions must all be true:

  1. Team gate — the team’s total pre-qualified for the week reaches the Weekly Bonus Target (members ÷ 700).
  2. Be active that week — you had some activity (campaign members, a warm transfer as caller, or a pre-qualified lead as texter or caller). Anyone out the entire week does not qualify for that week’s bonus.
  3. Juan is excluded — as manager, he does not draw the team weekly bonus; his pay is handled separately on the manager page.

6 · Weekly Bonus $

When the team clears the weekly gate, every active team member earns a flat bonus for that week:

  • $16 for the week if the team hit Top Target (members ÷ 600)
  • $15 for the week if the team hit Optimal (members ÷ 700) but not Top
  • $0 if the team is below Optimal

Your Weekly Bonus $ = the sum of these weekly amounts across every week you were active and the team cleared the gate.

It’s a flat amount per person per qualifying week — not per member.

7 · Star of the Month

Each month, the top sourcer (phone) who got the highest Pre-Qualified leads wins Star of the Month and a $50 bonus.

  • Winner = the person with the most pre-qualified leads for the whole month (counted month-to-date).
  • The winner’s name and the $50 are revealed only on the last calendar day of the month, once the month is final.

8 · Potential Star of the Month

Before the month’s last day, the dashboard shows a live standing instead of a winner:

  • The top 2 people by month-to-date pre-qualified count, with their running counts.
  • No money is awarded yet — it’s just who’s ahead. On the last day of the month the leader becomes the official Star and receives the $50.

9 · Own Earnings (Juan)

Juan’s own earnings come from the leads he personally works — using the same bands as everyone else (sections 1 & 2):

  • His Pre-Qualify $ — the text and phone shares from leads he sourced.
  • Plus Star of the Month ($50) if he wins it that month.

He does not draw the weekly team bonus — as manager he is excluded from it (section 5).

10 · Team Override (15% / 18%)

On top of his own earnings, Juan earns an override on the rest of the team’s pre-qualify production:

Override = a percentage of the other reps’ combined Pre-Qualify $ (the whole team’s pre-qualify pay, minus Juan’s own).

The rate depends on how the team did that period:

  • 15% — the standard rate.
  • 18% — when the team hits Top Target for the period (team pre-qualified ≥ members ÷ 600).

Manager Payout — when it unlocks

Juan’s Payout = Own Earnings + Team Override.

The payout only unlocks when the team hits quota: every week in the selected period must clear its Weekly Bonus Target (members ÷ 700). Until every week clears, the payout stays locked and the panel shows what it “would be” once the team hits quota.

Week & Month Boundaries

Weeks run Monday–Sunday, and each week counts toward the month that its Monday falls in. So when the end of one month and the start of the next land in the same week, that whole week is counted in the month of its Monday: a week that starts on the last Monday of the previous month is that month’s final week, even though it includes the first days of the new month. (This affects the weekly bonus weeks and the Star-of-the-Month window.)
